Education

I picked up Java in highschool as part of taking AP Computer Science. Maybe not the easiest language to understand at the time, but I was motivated enough to take on projects like custom Minecraft Bukkit plugins and from-scratch Entity Component Systems and Game Engines. Most projects I took on had me in way over my head, so naturally, I was rarely able to handle the complexity of the problems I took on. I did end up learning tons from these experiences as I became exposed to Software Design principles, OOP, and other methodologies for tackling complexity.

Upon entering college, I got into competitive algorithmic problem solving. While I wasn't terribly well-ranked, it did help me improve my problem solving skills and exposed me to subjects like Computational Geometry, Dynamic Programming, and other problem domains. I got a scholarship for my involvement in the school's competitive programming team which helped me in getting my Bachelor's in Computer Science.

My favorite classes from my program included Computer Graphics, Theory of Algorithms, Principles of Programming Languages, and Computer Networking.

Career

While in my first year of college, I joined Ultimate Software as an intern. I moved through a development team and a support team before I started my work in a DevOps-focused team. After interning for over 2 years, I finally joined full time before graduating from college.

My work at Ultimate focuses on developing and operating tooling and services that are consumed by internal development teams. I've written appliance configuration code in Chef, worked on an in-house deployment platform using Python and Terraform, and collaborated heavily on our first internal Kubernetes offering to keep parity with our public cloud offerings.

In my time at Ultimate, I've taken leadership training, become a certified Google Cloud Architect, participated in a few hackathons, and attended HashiConf. I'd like to continue advancing my career in Site Reliability Engineering but am also open to other career fields in my interests.
